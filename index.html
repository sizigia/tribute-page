Paulo Freire

Brazilian educator best known for helping learners overcome oppression and achieve various kinds of freedom

Paulo Freire's life timeline:

  • 1921 - Born into a middle-class family in Recife, Brazil
  • 1929 - The Great Depression engulfs the world economy and he is pushed into a life of poverty and hunger
  • 1931 - He moves with his family to a less expensive city of Jaboatao dos Guararapes
  • 1933 - His father dies leaving his wife and children to fend for themselves
  • 1943 - His family’s fortunes improves and he is able to pursue higher education at the University of Recife. He studies law, philosophy, and the psychology of language
  • 1944 - Marries Elza Maia Costa de Oliveira, a teacher. They have five children and are happily married till Elza’s death in 1986.
  • 1946 - Is appointed the Director of the Department of Education and Culture of the Social Service in the state of Pernambuco
  • 1961 - Becomes the director of the Department of Cultural Extension of Recife University
  • 1962 - Becomes involved in an educational project aimed at dealing with mass illiteracy
  • 1963 - The government draws up a plan to establish 2000 cultural circles to reach 2 000 000 illiterates
  • 1964 - A military coup brings the existing regime to an end and he is imprisoned for 70 days following the coup
  • 1967 - Publishes his first book ‘Education as the Practice of Freedom’
  • 1969 - Is offered a visiting professorship at Harvard University
  • 1970 - Is appointed the special educational advisor to the Department of Education, World Congress of Churches in Geneva, Switzerland
  • 1980 - Is made the supervisor of adult literacy project for the Workers’ Party in Sao Paulo, Brazil
  • 1997 - Dies of heart failure in at the age of 75

― Paulo Freire, Pedagogy of the Oppressed
